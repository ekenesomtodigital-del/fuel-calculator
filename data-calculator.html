Smart Tip

Nigerian networks frequently run flash sales and special promotions. A bundle advertised as "₦1,000 for 1GB" might have a ₦500 version for the same data hidden in the app. Always calculate cost per GB before buying.

Why Cost per GB Matters Money Saver

₦ A ₦500/0.5GB bundle and a ₦1,000/1GB bundle both look "equal" — but the bigger plan may actually give you a worse deal if the network is running a promo elsewhere.
📊 Nigerian data plans vary wildly. Glo often leads on raw data volume, while MTN offers better coverage. Comparing ₦/GB helps you separate coverage quality from bundle value.
🎯 For small business owners and remote workers, tracking data cost per GB can save thousands of naira monthly — especially when tethering mobile data to a laptop.
📱 MB vs GB confusion is real. 1,000MB = 1GB. Some bundles list sizes in MB to make them appear bigger. SabiCalc handles both so you always know the truth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which Nigerian network gives the cheapest data? ▼
Based on current plans, Glo consistently offers the lowest cost per GB — often ₦200–₦250/GB for larger bundles. However, Glo's network quality and coverage is weaker than MTN in many areas, especially outside major cities. Airtel often balances price and coverage well. The "cheapest" depends on whether speed and reliability matter more than raw data size to you.
How much data do I need per month in Nigeria? ▼
It depends on your usage pattern. Light users (mainly WhatsApp, occasional browsing) need about 1–2 GB/month. Average users (social media, YouTube, work calls) typically need 5–10 GB/month. Heavy users who stream video daily or tether data to a laptop can consume 20–50 GB/month or more.
What is a good cost per GB in Nigeria? ▼
A great deal is anything below ₦300/GB — typically found on Glo or during network promotions. Average value is ₦300–₦600/GB, which covers most standard MTN, Airtel, and 9mobile bundles. Anything above ₦700/GB is generally a poor deal and you should look for a larger bundle or check for active promotions.
Can I use this calculator for MB-sized bundles? ▼
Yes. For MB bundles, convert to GB first: divide the MB value by 1000. For example, 500MB = 0.5 GB. Enter 0.5 in the "Data Size" field. You can also enter decimal values like 0.25 for 250MB bundles.
Do Nigerian networks run flash data sales? ▼
Yes — and frequently. MTN, Airtel, and Glo all run time-limited promotions, especially on their official apps (MyMTN, Airtel Thanks, Glo Café). These can offer 2–3× normal data volume at the same price. Always check the app before buying a bundle the traditional way.
