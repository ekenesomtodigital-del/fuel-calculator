Frequently Asked Questions

How much fuel does Lagos to Ibadan use? ▼
The Lagos–Ibadan expressway is approximately 131 km. A typical small car doing 13 km/litre would consume about 10 litres. At ₦930/litre, that's roughly ₦9,300 one-way. An SUV doing 9 km/litre would use about 14.5 litres — around ₦13,500.
What is a good fuel consumption rate for Nigerian roads? ▼
On Nigerian highways, a small petrol car typically achieves 11–15 km/litre. City driving in Lagos or Abuja traffic can drop this to 7–10 km/litre due to stop-and-go conditions. SUVs and buses consume significantly more. If you know your own car's consumption, entering it manually gives the most accurate result.
How do I find out my car's fuel consumption? ▼
Fill your tank completely. Reset your trip odometer to zero. Drive normally until you need fuel again. Refill and note how many litres you used. Divide the kilometres driven by litres used. For example: 300 km ÷ 25 litres = 12 km/litre. This is your real-world fuel consumption.
Does this calculator work for diesel vehicles? ▼
Yes. Simply enter your vehicle's diesel consumption in the "Fuel Consumption" field and use the current diesel price per litre as your fuel price. Diesel prices in Nigeria are usually different from petrol prices, so always check the current rate at the filling station.
Why does my result say to budget extra? ▼
Real-world driving involves variables like traffic jams, AC usage, road gradients, and vehicle load. These factors can increase actual fuel consumption by 10–25% above the theoretical estimate. SabiCalc adds a buffer recommendation so you're not stranded on the highway with an empty tank.
